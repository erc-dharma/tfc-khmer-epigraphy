VI. [metre: anuṣṭubh, pāda c sa-vipulā : - - - ⏑ ⏑ ⏑ - - ]
(11) - - - kr̥panaṁ dr̥ṣṭvā yaṁ kauravasuyodhanāḥ
(12) vandīnān tu navayuddhe nemur na tu yudhiṣṭhiraṁ ||

VII. [metre: anuṣṭubh, pāda a na-vipulā : - - ⏑ - ⏑ ⏑ ⏑ - ]
(13) dhāmasthito vahudhiyā śaktyācalajayā mataḥ
(14) triśaktiśūlavān mānyo yaś śaṅkara ivāparaḥ ||

% triśaktiśūlavān : IC V, p. 178, n. 2 : Les deux premiers caractères sont douteux.
% Dominic Goodall : One suspects that Cœdès has not fully interpreted the puns here: śaktyā acalajayā, in one meaning, must be 'by his "Sakti Pārvatī'; in another, it may be his `spear of adamant'; triśakti may refer to the 3 royal powers: mantra-, prabhu-, utsāha; śūla perhaps refers to taxation; as for dhāma, it may refer also to the king's palace.

VIII. [metre: anuṣṭubh]

(16) gurus suyodhanas samā nemus te yan dhanāñjayam ||

kr̥pācāryyaḥ : IC V, p. 178, n. 3 : Les deux premiers caractères sont douteux.

(21) vrahmarṣeḥ kamvunāmnā yas sa vabhūva kulodvahaḥ

XII. [metre: anuṣṭubh]
(23) dharmmakṣayakr̥to dharmmī vrahmarṣiḥ kamvuvaṅśajaḥ

XIII. [metre: anuṣṭubh]
(25) tejasā yo tidīptena jagat pātun tapo karot 
(26) dagdhum eva tapasyan tam aurvan vidvo hasann iva ||

% vīdvo is the reading in IC V, p. 179.  What the estampage n.979 has is unclear to me, but the sense requires vidvo (from vidvas).

XIV. [metre: anuṣṭubh]
(27) vācaspatir dhiyā bhūmiḥ kṣamayā dhairyato calaḥ 
(28) gāmbhīryād amvudhir dānair nidhīśo yo mahātapāḥ ||

XV. [metre: anuṣṭubh]
(29) śekur vyājai striyā hantum anityau vairiṇau surāḥ 
(30) tān hasann iva dhīdīptyā śatrūn nityāñ jigāya yaḥ ||

XVI. [metre: anuṣṭubh]
(31) kṣīrāśī yo mahātejā pūjyaś śrīkamvubhūbhr̥tām 
(32) heman nāgamukhīn dolāṁ yasmai so dāt mahīdharaḥ ||

% dāt : IC V, p. 179, n. 1 : Sic, corr. : dād. 
% Cœdès translates : "Ce roi buveur de lait, plein de majesté, digne du respect des rois porteurs de la terre de Çrī Kambu, lui donna un palanquin d'or à tête de nāga." But it is rather the sage Kamvu who nourishes himself from milk alone, as we learn in stanza X.  All becomes smooth, however, if we assume that the engraver mistakenly wrote yasmai for tasmai (perhaps under the influence of stanzas XVIII and XIX below.  This would allow us to interpret : "To this milk-drinker of great power, revered by the kings of the territory of Kamvu, the king gave a golden snake-headed swing/palanquin."

XVII. [metre: anuṣṭubh]
(33) viṣayan nyastaśeṣākhyaṁ samr̥ddhaṁ rāṣṭrapūritam 
(34) yatrāsitānadīpāraṁ sa dadau bhūpatīśvaraḥ ||

XVIII. [metre: anuṣṭubh]
(35) cāmīkaraṁ samudgena karaṅkaṅ kamalāṅkitam 
(36) nityotphultaṁ śriyaḥ padman dadau yasmai sa bhūpatiḥ ||

% Cœdès proposes correcting nityotphultaṁ to nityotphullaṁ (IC V, p. 179, n. 2); but utphulta is also attested. Cf. the metallic lotuses given in K. 1320 !

XIX. [metre: anuṣṭubh]
(37) hemadaṇdaṁ sitacchatraṁ māyūrañ chatram uttamaṁ 
(38) tāraṁ pratigrahaṁ yasmai prādād avanīndrādhipaḥ ||

% Cœdès interprets tāraṁ pratigrahaṁ to mean "une perle et un crachoir"; but it seems more likely that a śilver spittoon" was intended.
