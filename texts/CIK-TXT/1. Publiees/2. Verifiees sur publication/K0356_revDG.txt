VI. [śārdūlavikrīḍita]
(11) yoddhre yuddhasamuddhatāya ripave divyaṁ sadivyāṅganan
dātā lokam ihodayan natikr̥te yo raṇyam ājer drute
(12) uddhartur dharaṇīdharasya dharaṇīm ambhodhimagnāṁ purā
līlāṁ lipsur ivoddadhāra patitān tāṁ viplavāvdhau punaḥ ||
% In the second pāda, Cœdès splits ihodayann atikr̥te, which is grammatically
% possible, but leaves him unable to account for atikr̥te, as he explains in the note to his
% translation.  Splitting in this way (ihodayan natikr̥te), however, enables us to 
% understand that the king gave the sky with its celestial nymphs (in other words death)
% to enemies who continued to fight (yoddhre), that he gave the world and prosperity
% in this world to enemies who prostrated themselves (natikr̥te 
% [dative singular of natikr̥t]), and that he gave the wilderness to those who fled 
% (drute [locative singular in the sense of a dative]) from the battlefield (DG).

VII. [sragdharā]
(13) kāman dagdhvā tadaṅgadyutinikhiladhanāny ātmasātkr̥tya gātre
kīrttirbhūtārddhacandro ripujanabhayakr̥t kālakūṭāgryavīryyaḥ
(14) gaṅgāmbhassuprasādo yuganayanabhavadvahnitejā nu jātaś
śarvvo sāv īśvaro yas sakalaguṇanidhis sāmvudhiṁ kṣmāṁ arakṣat ||
% In the second pāda, Cœdès notes that we should understand kīrttibhūtā@
% in place of kīrttirbhūtā@; but from the point of view of both sense and metre,
% it would be better to assume that what was intended was rather kīrttībhūtā@ (DG).
% In the third pāda, Cœdès prints @vahnitejānujātaś, and he
% notes that we should understand @tejonujātaś;
% but this may not be the best solution.  Instead, we should probably understand
% ayuganayanabhavadvahnitejā nu jātaś (the initial a would of course have disappeared
% in sandhi with the previous word), "[and] having the fieriness of the flames that 
% arise from Him-with-an-odd-number-of-eyes, surely (nu) he has come into being (jātaḥ)
% [as a second IIśvara...]"

VIII. [āryā]
(15) tasyāgrajo dhr̥tāsīr yyudhi vairigaṇair udīrito ntāgniḥ

% Instead of dhr̥tāsīr, more correct would be dhr̥tāsir (no change in the metre).

IX. [śārdūlavikrīḍita]
(16) preṅkhadkhadgabhr̥tānujena jayinā yas tena yuddhe yuto
durdharṣo rigaṇair ivāmarapatiś śrījāninā sāriṇā
(17) yatkāruṇyasuvr̥ṣṭihr̥ṣṭahr̥dayāny etāni śuṣkāny api
prārūḍhāni punaḥ phalanti ca jagatsasyāni bhāntā āyugāt ||
% In the first pāda, we should understand preṅkhatkhaḍga@.
% In the final pāda, the text as printed by Cœdès is unmetrical.  We should

% śhining until the [end of the] eon" 
% or "[and] they'll shine until [the end of] the eon".
